Timeline of the Israel-Palestine Conflict

The long-standing conflict has resulted in numerous clashes and disputes over the years.

By Khushali Haji

The Israeli-Palestinian conflict is a long-standing and deeply rooted dispute that has spanned decades, resulting in suffering and loss of life for both sides. The long-standing conflict is a deeply complex issue with historical, political, and social dimensions.

It has resulted in numerous clashes and disputes over the years, dating back to the early 20th century. The conflict has witnessed a series of significant events and developments that have shaped its trajectory, Saturday's escalation being the latest. It's consequences continue to reverberate in the region and on the global stage. Below is a brief timeline of major events that have happened in the last few decades and their major consequences.

Till the Early 20th Century

1799: Napoleon offers Palestine as a Homeland to the Jews.

1885: The term "zionism" is coined and 2 years later, the first zionist organization is founded.

1917: Balfour Declaration - The British government expresses support for the establishment of a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine or Arab land.

Consequence: Set the stage for Jewish immigration and tensions with the Arab population.

1940s

1940s: Fighting breaks out between Palestinians and Jewish militias.

1947: United Nations Partition Plan - The UN approves a plan to partition Palestine into separate Jewish and Arab states.

Consequence: The plan is rejected by Arab states, leading to the first Arab-Israeli war.

1948: Establishment of the State of Israel - Israel declares independence, leading to the Arab-Israeli War.

Consequence: Palestinian displacement (Nakba) and ongoing hostilities.

1950s-1960s

1967: Six-Day War - Israel launched pre-emptive attacks on Egypt, Syria and Jordan, after Egypt built up forces in Sinai and closed the Suez Canal to Israeli shipping. Israel captures the West Bank, Gaza Strip, and other territories, including control of more than 1 million Palestinians.

Consequence: Ongoing occupation, settlements, and Palestinian resistance.

1980s-1990s

1987-1993: First Intifada - A Palestinian uprising against Israeli occupation.

Consequence: Widespread violence and protest, increased international attention and eventual Oslo Accords between the PLO (Palestine Liberation Organisation) and Israel.

1993: Oslo Accords - Israel and the PLO agree on a framework for peace.

Consequence: Creation of the Palestinian Authority and limited self-governance in parts of the West Bank and Gaza.

2000s

2000-2005: Second Intifada - A violent Palestinian uprising after Israeli prime minister visited the Temple Mount.

Consequence: Breakdown of peace negotiations. Increased Israeli security measures and the construction of the West Bank barrier.

2010s

2014: Gaza War - A series of conflict between Israel and Hamas in Gaza.

Consequence: Death and displacement, damaged infrastructure, and ongoing tensions.

2017: U.S. Recognition of Jerusalem - The U.S. recognizes Jerusalem as Israel's capital.

Consequence: Widespread protests and diplomatic tensions.

2020s

2020: Abraham Accords - Israel signs normalization agreements with several Arab states.

Consequence: Shifts in regional alliances and decreased Palestinian leverage.

2021: Gaza Conflict - Renewed violence in Gaza.

Consequence: Temporary ceasefires, but underlying issues remain.

2023: The conflict escalates: Hamas launched the biggest attack on Israel in years from the Gaza Strip in October.

Consequence: Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu declared that his country was 'at a war' with Hamas.
